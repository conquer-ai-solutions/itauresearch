The Core Constraint
Why AI Cannot Touch Data Sources Directly
Itaú BBA Research relies on licensed data providers — Bloomberg, S&P Capital IQ, Economatica — for the financial data that underpins every research report. These providers have specific contractual terms about how their data can be used, and most explicitly prohibit AI agents from connecting to their systems. This single fact shapes every design decision in our architecture.
Licensing Reality
Capital IQ (S&P): Explicitly prohibits AI agents, MCP servers, and automated AI-driven systems from connecting to their APIs. Bearer tokens expire every 60 minutes and cannot be issued to AI processes.

Bloomberg: AI/ML use requires a specific license amendment. Standard terminal or BLPAPI licences do not permit feeding data into AI systems.

Economatica: Commercial license with distribution restrictions. Terms must be reviewed for AI-derived-output use cases.

The implication: Any architecture where an LLM connects directly to these systems is non-compliant and puts Itaú at licensing risk.
What's at stake: If an AI system connects directly to Bloomberg or Capital IQ, Itaú risks license revocation, financial penalties, and loss of access to data the research team depends on daily. The architecture must enforce a hard boundary between data ingestion and AI consumption — not as a policy document that people follow, but as network-level infrastructure where the AI physically cannot reach the source systems.
Solution Architecture
Decoupled Two-Layer Design
The system is split into two completely separate layers. Layer 1 (left) handles all data ingestion — traditional ETL pipelines that pull from Bloomberg, Capital IQ, public regulators, etc. A data lake (center) stores everything in normalized tables. Layer 2 (right) is where the AI lives — it can only read from the lake, never from source systems. The red line between them is a network-enforced firewall.

Unified Data Lake
Schema Design & Provenance
Today, each data source uses its own format, field names, and identifiers. Bloomberg calls it "Sales", Capital IQ calls it "Total Revenue", CVM uses Portuguese labels. An analyst mentally reconciles these every time they build a model. Our data lake normalizes everything into 6 common tables — so Bloomberg's revenue figure and CIQ's revenue figure for the same company sit side by side, tagged by source. The AI reads one clean schema instead of navigating nine different formats.
Provenance means every single record in the lake carries a tag showing exactly which source it came from, when it was pulled, what license covers it, and a hash of the original data. This isn't just for audit — it's how the AI cites its sources in every output.

AI Research Engine
What Happens Inside the 1M-Token Window
Most AI systems use "RAG" (retrieval-augmented generation) — they search for small snippets of relevant data and feed those to the model. The problem with RAG for equity research is that analysts need to reason across many data points simultaneously: comparing a company's financials with peer benchmarks, overlaying macro trends, checking internal prior work. RAG loses that cross-referencing ability.
Our approach is different: we load the entire financial universe for a company — financials, filings, market data, sector macro, internal docs — into a single 1M-token context window. The AI sees everything at once, just like an analyst with every terminal and document open on their desk. No chunking, no information loss.

Enterprise & Compliance
Governance, Security & Regulatory Alignment
As Brazil's largest private bank, Itaú operates under three key regulatory frameworks: BACEN (Banco Central do Brasil — the central bank regulator, equivalent to the Fed), CVM (Comissão de Valores Mobiliários — Brazil's securities regulator, equivalent to the SEC), and LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados — Brazil's data protection law, equivalent to GDPR). Any AI system processing financial data within Itaú must satisfy all three.
Data Provenance & Audit Trail
Every record: source, timestamp, license, SHA256 hash. AI outputs cite sources inline. Any claim traceable in 3 hops. "Did AI access Bloomberg directly?" → No, with full lineage proof.
Data Residency & Perimeter
Lake + ETL + AI all deploy within Itaú's AWS VPC (sa-east-1, São Paulo). No data leaves the perimeter. Licensed data stays within existing contract terms. Bedrock invocations stay in-region.
LGPD Compliance
System processes financial/market data, not personal data — minimal LGPD exposure. Internal docs processed within VPC. No personal data used for model training. Audit logs on every access.
BACEN / CVM Regulatory
AI outputs marked as AI-assisted, not autonomous. Analyst remains author. Aligns with CVM Instruction 20 (research independence). All outputs include source attribution for regulatory review.

Data Lake

🔒 Network isolation enforces the AI boundary. ETL can reach sources but not Bedrock. AI can reach the lake and Bedrock but not sources. License compliance is a network property, not a trust-the-code property.
